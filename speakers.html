Invited Speakers

EurIPS 2025 Workshop: Foundation Models for Multimodal Health Signals

Keynote Speaker

Prof. Sarah Johnson

Stanford University

Topic: The Future of Multimodal Foundation Models in Healthcare

Prof. Johnson is a leading researcher in AI for healthcare, focusing on multimodal learning for clinical applications. Her work has pioneered several breakthroughs in integrating medical imaging with clinical text data.

Invited Speakers

Dr. Michael Chen

Harvard Medical School

Topic: Bridging Clinical and Genomic Data with Foundation Models

Dr. Chen's research focuses on integrating multi-omics data with clinical information using foundation models to advance precision medicine.

Dr. Emily Rodriguez

Massachusetts Institute of Technology

Topic: Privacy-Preserving Multimodal Health Models

Dr. Rodriguez is an expert in privacy-preserving machine learning techniques for healthcare applications, with a focus on federated learning and differential privacy.

Dr. James Wilson

Food and Drug Administration (FDA)

Topic: Regulatory Considerations for AI in Healthcare

Dr. Wilson works at the intersection of AI regulation and healthcare innovation, helping shape policies for the safe and effective deployment of AI in clinical settings.

Prof. David Lee

University of Cambridge

Role: Panel Moderator

Prof. Lee's research focuses on interpretable AI for healthcare, with a particular emphasis on clinical decision support systems and their integration into clinical workflows.
